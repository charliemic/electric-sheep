## Risks of Changing License

### 1. Legal Risks ‚ö†Ô∏è
- **Contributor Rights**: Previous contributors may have rights to their contributions
- **Existing Users**: Users who already have MIT-licensed code may continue using it
- **Forked Projects**: Others may have forked your project under MIT
- **Complexity**: Requires legal expertise and contributor permission

### 2. Community Risks ‚ö†Ô∏è
- **Loss of Contributors**: Open-source community may stop contributing
- **Reputation Impact**: May be seen as "selling out" or "taking back" open-source
- **Forking and Competition**: Community may fork last MIT version and continue development

### 3. Business Risks ‚ö†Ô∏è
- **Market Perception**: May be seen as less "open" or "community-friendly"
- **Competitive Disadvantage**: Open-source forks may compete
- **Legal Costs**: $2,000-$10,000+ for legal review

### Why Keep MIT License

1. ‚úÖ **Already Allows Commercial Use** - No license change needed
